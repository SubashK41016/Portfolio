The project is designed to detect fake social media accounts and prevent misuse of digital identities.

Problem Statement

In today's world, almost everyone uses mobile phones and social media platforms like Instagram and Facebook. Due to this widespread usage, fake accounts are increasingly created for pranks, harassment, spreading inappropriate content, fraudulent activities, and cyber crimes.

Major issues highlighted:

  • Trust issues in social media
  • Spreading inappropriate content
  • Loss of life due to cyber harassment
  • Cyber bullying and harassment
  • No proper response to user reports

Working Concept

Basic Requirements

Collects legitimate details like Username, Password, Date of Birth, Gender, Mobile number, and Profile picture during account creation.

IMEI Tracking

Records the unique IMEI number of the user's mobile device. Identifies when multiple IDs are created from the same device.

Smart Surveillance

Accounts with multiple IDs from the same IMEI come under surveillance. Text monitoring increases only if involved in suspicious activities.

Monitoring & Action System

If an ID is involved in unwanted activities, a structured action plan is initiated:

  • ⚠️ First Warning: An initial alert is sent to the user.
  • ⚠️ Second Warning: A subsequent alert if behavior continues.
  • ⚠️ Third Warning: Final warning before severe action.
  • ❌ Permanent Suspension: All IDs associated with that IMEI are permanently suspended without recovery option.
  • 📤 Legal Reporting: A report is sent to the concerned department for further legal action.

Target Customers

The primary customers for this software include Instagram, Facebook, Meta, and other social media companies. TRAFIS helps platforms maintain trust and improve security against fake accounts that damage company reputation, increase privacy concerns, and lead to stricter government regulations and legal challenges.

Legal Aspects & Solutions

The project references legal consequences, such as Section 420 of IPC (India), where fraud can lead to up to 7 years imprisonment. It proposes solutions like confiscating fake IDs, refusing service upon suspicion, and strengthening scanning engines to detect fake IDs more accurately compared to weak 2D barcode systems.
